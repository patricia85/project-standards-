Adaptive Development Followed by Predictive Rollout

The early processes use an adaptive development life cycle, which is then followed by a predictive rollout phase.

Strategy

Use when innovation and exploration are needed initially, but a structured implementation is required later. Particularly useful for new product development with uncertain requirements but fixed delivery dates.

Approach Composition

Adaptive 50%
Predictive 50%

Recommended Use Cases

  • New product launches
  • Market testing followed by full release
  • R&D projects
  • Systems with uncertain requirements

Benefits

  • Flexibility in early stages
  • Structured delivery
  • Reduced risk in implementation
  • Clear transition point

Challenges

  • Transition management
  • Documentation handover
  • Team restructuring needed
  • Cultural shift required

About PMI Disciplined Agile®

The PMI Disciplined Agile® (DA®) tool kit describes three levels of hybrid approaches commonly found in projects across industries. These levels represent different balances between predictive and adaptive methodologies, allowing organizations to tailor their approach based on project needs and organizational context.

Each level offers a different distribution of adaptive and predictive elements, enabling teams to choose the right balance for their specific project requirements, organizational constraints, and team capabilities.
